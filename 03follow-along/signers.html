Who Were the Signers

Most Famous Signers

Thomas Jefferson
Principal author of the Declaration of Independence.
3rd President of the United States.
Seen as one of the intellectual architects of American democracy; associated with the Louisiana Purchase and founding the University of Virginia.
John Adams
A leading voice pushing for independence in the Continental Congress.
2nd President of the United States; first Vice President under George Washington.
Major Federalist figure and key diplomat during the Revolution.
Benjamin Franklin
Celebrated polymath, diplomat, inventor, and elder statesman.
Ambassador to France; key in securing French support for the Revolution.
Often considered the most universally admired American of the 18th century.
John Hancock
President of the Second Continental Congress.
Known for his large, bold signature on the Declaration.
His name is now a synonym for a signature ("Put your John Hancock here").
First Governor of Massachusetts after independence.
Samuel Adams
Early and passionate advocate for independence; leader of the Sons of Liberty.
Cousin to John Adams.
Key organizer of resistance to British policy, including the Boston Tea Party.
Roger Sherman
Only person to sign all four great state papers of the U.S. (Continental Association, Declaration of Independence, Articles of Confederation, Constitution).
Robert Morris
Financier of the American Revolution; played a crucial role in funding the Continental Army.
Richard Henry Lee
Proposed the resolution for independence in the Continental Congress that led to the drafting of the Declaration.
